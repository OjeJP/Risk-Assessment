Key findings & recommendations to management

IT Servers require priority hardening

Staff training on IT policies and SSL/TLS deployment were identified as the most impactful immediate treatments for the highest-scoring risk asset (mark 10/25).

Database security demands off-site backups and patching

CVE-2022-46763 (SQL injection via stored function) requires both a new patch deployment and segregated off-site backup strategy to satisfy A.12.3.1 and A.12.4.x.

Physical controls are the overlooked gap

Server Room and Backup Server Room each scored 5/5 on vulnerability severity but low on likelihood. Annex A.11.1 physical controls should be implemented pre-emptively given the catastrophic potential business impact.

Management review cycle needed

Recommended a continuous review process covering residual risk status, legislative changes, and new asset onboarding — particularly relevant given the company's aviation tech expansion roadmap.

02

Boeing Information Security Policy — Critical Analysis

Boeing's IS Policy: Securing Airline Information on Ground and in Air

Course: Information Security Laws & Regulations  ·  ITMO University, 2023

ISO 27001 ISO 27002 DMARC / Email Security Supply Chain Risk Policy Analysis

This essay critically evaluated Boeing's information security policy framework for protecting airline data in both ground and airborne environments. The analysis benchmarked the policy against ISO/IEC 27001:2013 and ISO/IEC 27002:2013, identifying both strengths and material gaps with actionable improvement recommendations.

Policy strengths — what Boeing gets right

Alignment with ISO 27001 scope and risk identification

The policy correctly identifies risks to information assets and defines the ISMS scope in line with Clause 4 of ISO 27001, providing a structured management foundation.

Broad coverage across core security domains

Data classification, access control, incident management, staff training, encryption requirements, and secure communication channels are all addressed — reflecting the ISO 27002 control domains.

24/7 global ethics line

A third-party managed, always-on ethics and incident reporting line available across 35 countries demonstrates commitment to operational security beyond technical controls.

Critical gaps & vulnerabilities identified

Test development networks publicly exposed

Despite a stated requirement to encrypt all data, Boeing's test development networks were publicly reachable via the internet — potentially exposing software source code and build systems to sophisticated adversaries. This is a direct contradiction of the policy's own encryption mandate.

Email infrastructure compromised — no DMARC enforcement

An email server was found infected with multiple malware strains. Critically, the boeing.com domain lacked DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting and Conformance), leaving employees, suppliers, and government clients vulnerable to email spoofing and targeted phishing campaigns.

No third-party vendor security policy

The ethics line is managed entirely by an external party, yet the policy contains no documented procedure for managing incidents involving third-party vendors or partners — a significant supply chain security gap given Boeing's extensive contractor ecosystem.

Training programme lacks specificity

While employee training is acknowledged, the policy fails to specify the type, target audience, frequency, or content of required training — making compliance measurement and effectiveness evaluation impossible.

Recommendations

Network Segmentation

Isolate test/dev networks from public internet exposure using air-gapping or strict perimeter controls.

DMARC / DKIM / SPF

Deploy full email authentication stack on boeing.com to prevent domain spoofing and protect all downstream email recipients.

Vendor Risk Programme

Formalize a third-party security policy covering incident response, contractual obligations, and regular audits.

Training Framework

Define role-based training curricula, delivery cadence, and measurable completion criteria aligned to ISO 27002 §6.3.
